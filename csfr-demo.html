CSRF allows attackers to trick a logged‚Äëin user‚Äôs browser into performing unintended actions. Browsers automatically attach cookies to requests, even if the request originated from another site.

3. Why CSRF Happens

3.1 Browser Behaviour

Browsers automatically attach cookies to matching domain requests ‚Äî even across sites.

8. Summary / Key Takeaways

  • CSRF exploits browser behaviour, not server bugs
  • Tokens break forged requests by requiring a secret attackers cannot know
  • SameSite cookies dramatically reduce risk
  • CSRF must be paired with authentication hardening
